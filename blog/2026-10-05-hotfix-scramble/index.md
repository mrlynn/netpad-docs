## Why This Happens

Organizations create process for good reasons:

- **Code review** catches bugs before production
- **Approval workflows** ensure proper authorization
- **Change management** maintains audit trails
- **Deployment queues** prevent conflicts

But standard processes assume standard timelines. Hotfixes need:

- **Speed** - Minutes matter
- **Focus** - Minimal review of minimal change
- **Access** - Right people, right permissions, right now
- **Documentation** - After, not before

When emergency procedures don't exist, teams either follow normal process (too slow) or bypass everything (too risky). Neither is good.

## The NetPad Approach

NetPad supports rapid iteration when needed:

- **Instant changes** - Modify forms and workflows without deployment
- **Version history** - Track what changed, when, by whom
- **Quick rollback** - Undo changes instantly if they make things worse
- **Environment promotion** - Fast path from fix to production
- **Audit trail** - Document what happened, even in emergencies

Make the fix. It's live immediately. If it's wrong, roll back. Document afterward. The platform supports both careful process and emergency response.
